## What our tone of voice is — and why it matters

HelloPrint exists to make print easier for everyone, and that mission has to come through in every word we write. Our customers are busy business owners, not print specialists. They're placing an order between two other things on their to-do list — and what they need from us is confidence, not complexity. Our tone of voice is the way we earn that trust before the product even arrives. It's how we sound when a first-time buyer is nervously uploading their first design, when a heavy user needs a bulk quote in under two minutes, and when a freelance designer is relying on us to not let their client down. In every one of those moments, we need to feel like the most competent, most approachable person in the room — someone who knows exactly what they're doing and makes you feel like you do too.

That's why HelloPrint's voice lands on three words: friendly, pragmatic, and expert. Not friendly in a way that's hollow or performative — but genuinely warm, like a colleague who happens to know everything about print. Not expert in a way that talks down or shows off — but quietly confident, the kind of confidence that comes from actually knowing what you're doing. And pragmatic because our customers don't have time for filler. They want the answer. They want to know the price, the lead time, and what to do next. Our job is to give them that — clearly, directly, and without making them feel like they had to work for it. When we get the tone right, a HelloPrint page feels effortless to read. When we get it wrong, it either sounds like every other ecommerce site, or like a sales pitch — and neither of those is who we are.

## The HelloPrint voice character

If HelloPrint were a person, he'd be a man in his early thirties — someone who started his own business at 26, figured out most of it by doing, and now genuinely enjoys helping others do the same. He's not a print nerd who talks in specs. He's not a salesman who tells you you need something you don't. He's the friend who happens to know everything about print — the one you call when you need 500 flyers for Saturday and you're not sure where to start. He gives you a straight answer, finds you the best deal, and makes sure the thing actually shows up on time.

His voice is calm and confident — never rushed, never pushy. He speaks clearly because he respects your time, not because he's trying to impress you. He'll crack a joke if the moment calls for it, but he won't force it. When something goes wrong, he owns it and fixes it. When something goes well, he's genuinely pleased for you — not in a hollow "great news!" way, but in the way someone is when they actually cared about the outcome.

**In casting terms:**

- **Gender:** Male
- **Age:** 28–38
- **Energy:** Grounded, warm, assured — never hyper, never flat
- **Accent / register:** Clear and accessible — educated but not posh, local but not regional
- **Pace:** Measured — lets ideas land, doesn't rush to fill silence
- **Feel:** The knowledgeable mate, not the corporate spokesperson

**What he sounds like in a radio spot:**

> *"Running a business means a hundred things at once. Your print shouldn't be one of them. HelloPrint — order today, delivered tomorrow."*

**What he doesn't sound like:**

> *"At HelloPrint, we're passionate about delivering world-class printing solutions tailored to your unique business needs!"*

## Default Tone

Friendly · Expert · Human

## By Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Transactional content | Clear, calm, structured |
| Marketing & ecommerce | Playful, confident, helpful |
| Customer service | Warm, direct, solution-first |
| OOH / billboard | Bold, short, ALL CAPS — 3–4 words max |
| Internal comms | Candid, concise, respectful |

## Writing Rules

- Lead with the main message immediately
- One core idea per section
- Short paragraphs and clear hierarchy
- Bullet points for scannability
- Clarity always beats cleverness

## Typography & Tone

| Font | Role | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Inter Bold | Headlines | Confident, short, informative |
| Inter Regular | Body | Explanatory, reassuring |
| Smiley (handwritten) | Greetings / sign-offs only | Warm, personal |

Never use handwritten or playful tone for prices, guarantees, or legal content.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Language & copy

| ✓ Do | ✗ Don't |
|---|---|
| "Order by 5pm, delivered tomorrow." | "Experience lightning-fast next-day fulfilment solutions." |
| "Not sure about your file? We'll check it for you." | "Leverage our state-of-the-art prepress verification technology." |
| "Your business cards, from €9.95." | "Unbeatable prices on a wide range of premium print products!" |
| "Something went wrong — let us fix it." | "We apologise for any inconvenience this may have caused." |
| "Need help? There's a real person here." | "Please consult our comprehensive FAQ documentation." |
| "20% off — use code POSTER20 at checkout." | "Don't miss this incredible limited-time saving opportunity!" |

### Headlines

| ✓ Do | ✗ Don't |
|---|---|
| "Make your walls work for you." | "Transform your space with our amazing wall print solutions!" |
| "Print made simple." | "Your one-stop-shop for all your printing needs." |
| "Your logo, on everything." | "Unlock endless customisation possibilities today." |
| "Fast. Affordable. Yours." | "Quality you can trust at prices you'll love!!!" |

### Tone in context

| ✓ Do | ✗ Don't |
|---|---|
| Warm when a customer is stuck: "Let's figure this out together." | Cold when a customer is stuck: "Please submit a support ticket." |
| Confident on quality: "Love it or we reprint it." | Timid on quality: "We try our best to ensure customer satisfaction." |
| Direct on price: "That's the price. No extras." | Vague on price: "Competitive pricing available on request." |
| Energetic on a win: "That order is on its way." | Hollow on a win: "Thank you for choosing HelloPrint for your printing requirements." |

### Never

- Use jargon or unexplained print terminology
- Sound salesy, aggressive, or overhyped
- Hide important information behind vague language
- Use exclamation marks more than once per page
- Write in passive voice when active is possible
- Start a sentence with "We are pleased to inform you"

## Values Connection

| Value | Tone behavior |
|---|---|
| Think Like A Customer | Plain language, no jargon, explain everything simply |
| Own It | Direct and confident — no watered-down copy |
| Do It Together | Warm and inclusive — never cold or corporate |
| Raise the Bar | Ambitious, never timid |
| Celebrate Success | Energetic when earned, never hollow |

## Capitalisation hard rule

The brand name is **HelloPrint** (one word, capital H, capital P). Never `Helloprint`, never `helloprint`, never `HELLOPRINT`, never `Hello Print`, never `Hello-Print`. The only exception is the legal entity `Helloprint B.V.` in footers, contracts, and invoices.
