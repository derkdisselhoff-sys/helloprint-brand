# HelloPrint Voice & Tone

Three words hold our voice together: **Friendly · Pragmatic · Expert**.

Friendly means we sound like a human, not a template. Pragmatic means we lead with the useful thing and trim the rest. Expert means we know print cold — and we never condescend about it.

## Default tone

When context is neutral (docs, product UI, internal Slack, standard email), default to **Friendly · Expert · Human**.

- Lead with the main message — never bury it under setup.
- One idea per section. If you need a second, start a new paragraph.
- Short paragraphs. Short sentences beat clever ones.
- Clarity beats cleverness. Always.

## Tone by context

| Context | Tone | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Transactional (order confirmations, invoices, receipts) | Clear · Calm · Structured | "Your order is in. We'll print, check, and ship it — you'll get tracking on Thursday." |
| Marketing (landing, campaigns, social) | Playful · Confident · Helpful | "Flyers that don't look like flyers. Sharp print, fair price, tomorrow on your desk." |
| Support (help docs, chat, email replies) | Warm · Direct · Solution-first | "Got it. Here's what I can do right now, and what'll take a day." |
| Internal (Slack, standup, docs) | Candid · Concise · Respectful | "Cart conversion dropped 2.1pp Tuesday. Pricing team is on it. Flagging so nobody duplicates the work." |

## Writing rules

**Do**

- Write like you're explaining it to a smart friend who isn't a print nerd.
- Use active voice. "We'll ship Thursday" not "Your order will be shipped on Thursday."
- Give the number. "48h delivery" beats "fast delivery."
- Name the person / team. "Sanne in Support can help" beats "our support team is available."
- Use everyday words where the industry word is cloudy. "Cardboard box" over "corrugated shipper" unless the reader is a buyer.
- Close loops. If you say "we'll confirm by Friday," confirm by Friday.

**Don't**

- Don't hedge. "We might be able to…" → "We can…" or "We can't, here's why."
- Don't pad. "At this moment in time" → "now." "Please be advised that" → cut it.
- Don't hype. "Revolutionary game-changing" is a red flag. Describe the result.
- Don't threaten. "You must…" / "Failure to…" → "Here's what happens if…" / "To avoid X, do Y."
- Don't apologise theatrically. Say it once, explain the fix, move on.
- Don't use idioms that don't travel. We're a European brand — "slam dunk" or "home run" mean nothing to half our readers.

## Words to avoid

- "Synergy", "leverage", "ecosystem" unless we genuinely mean the technical definition.
- "Best-in-class", "world-class", "revolutionary", "unparalleled" — prove it with a number instead.
- "As a valued customer" — nobody feels valued by being told they're valued.
- "Kindly" — overly formal, feels like a chain email.
- "Please find attached" — just say "Attached:" or "Here:".

## Values-to-tone mapping

Each value shows up in writing in a specific way:

- **Think Like A Customer** → Lead with what it means for them. "You get the PDF proof in 4 hours" before "We run a pre-press check."
- **Own It** → Use "I" and "we" in support. "I'll chase the supplier today" not "this has been escalated."
- **Do It Together** → Name who's in the room. "Product, Supply, and I agreed…" beats passive consensus-speak.
- **Raise the Bar** → Be specific about the improvement. "Cut turnaround from 72h to 48h" not "faster turnaround."
- **Celebrate Success** → Name the people. "Huge credit to Ana and Tomás for shipping this in a week."
- **AI-First Thinking** → If a process could be a skill, say so. "Automating this — adding to Claude" beats "taking it off your plate."

## Greetings and sign-offs

Friendly default — no theatre.

- "Hi {name}," / "Hey {name}," / "Hoi {name}," (NL)
- Sign off with your actual name. Not "Team HelloPrint" unless the reply truly isn't from a person.
- Smiley handwritten font is approved here and only here. Not in the body.

## Capitalisation (hard rule)

The brand name is **HelloPrint**. Always. This applies to every asset — emails, decks, subject lines, source code strings, SEO titles, Slack messages, voice-over scripts.

Forbidden forms: `Helloprint`, `helloprint`, `HELLOPRINT`, `Hello Print`, `Hello-Print`.

The only exception is the legal entity string — `Helloprint B.V.` — which appears in contracts and financial documents exactly as registered. Don't use it anywhere else.
